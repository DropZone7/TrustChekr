- **Business Account Package 2:** $20/month, 50 transactions
- Profit-sharing with members
- Modern digital banking platform
- Federal credit union ‚Äî available across Canada

### Coast Capital Savings
- BC-based, limited Ontario presence
- **Skip unless you move to Vancouver**

### Credit Union Verdict
Credit unions generally offer lower fees and profit-sharing, but weaker digital tools and smaller ATM networks. For a tech startup, the Big 5 or neo-banks are better fits unless you specifically value the community banking model.

## Documents & Setup Checklist

### What Alex Needs to Bring to Open a Business Bank Account

#### Required Documents for a Federal Corporation (CCPC)

1. **Certificate of Incorporation** (from Corporations Canada)
   - For 17734344 Canada Inc., this was issued when the federal corporation was registered
   
2. **Articles of Incorporation**
   - The founding document filed with Corporations Canada
   
3. **Corporate By-laws**
   - If adopted (standard set usually created at incorporation)
   
4. **Directors' Resolution / Corporate Resolution**
   - Authorizing the opening of the bank account
   - Specifying who has signing authority
   - Most banks provide a template form
   
5. **Notice of Directors** (or Annual Return showing current directors)
   - Lists all directors of the corporation
   
6. **Government-issued photo ID** for all directors/signing officers
   - Driver's licence or passport
   - Two pieces of ID typically required
   
7. **CRA Business Number (BN)**
   - **Critical:** Alex needs this. It's a 9-digit number assigned by CRA.
   - If the corporation was incorporated federally, Corporations Canada may have automatically requested a BN ‚Äî check the incorporation documents
   - If not assigned yet: Register at [CRA Business Registration Online](https://www.canada.ca/en/revenue-agency/services/tax/businesses/topics/registering-your-business/register.html) or call CRA at 1-800-959-5525
   - **Banks may open an account without it** but will need it shortly after
   
8. **Proof of business address**
   - Utility bill, lease, or bank statement showing business address
   - Home address is fine for a home-based startup

#### HST Registration
- **Required when:** Revenue exceeds $30,000 over four consecutive calendar quarters OR in a single calendar quarter
- **Voluntary registration:** Can register voluntarily at any time (recommended once you start incurring HST on business expenses ‚Äî you can claim Input Tax Credits)
- **How:** Through CRA Business Registration Online, same time as getting your BN
- **Recommendation:** Register for HST voluntarily from day one. You'll claim back HST on expenses (hosting, software, equipment) even before you have revenue.

#### Who Needs to Be Present
- All directors who will be signing officers on the account
- If Alex is the sole director, just Alex
- Bring **two pieces of government ID** (driver's licence + passport is ideal)

#### Pro Tips
- **Do the CRA BN registration first** ‚Äî it can take 1-2 weeks by mail, or same-day online
- **Open the bank account before spending anything** ‚Äî keeps corporate veil intact
- **Get a separate credit card** in the corporation's name immediately
- Some banks require a **minimum opening deposit** ($1 to $100 typically)

## Steve's Corner: Negotiation Tips for a Former Banker

Steve ‚Äî here's the inside playbook:

### 1. Timing Your Visit
- **Best time:** Tuesday to Thursday, mid-morning (10-11am). Avoid Mondays (busy) and Fridays (closings).
- **End of quarter** (March, June, Sept, Dec) ‚Äî advisors have targets to hit. More flexibility.
- **March is RIGHT NOW** ‚Äî Q1 end. Good timing.

### 2. What to Negotiate
- For a brand-new CCPC with no revenue, there's limited negotiation power. But:
  - **Ask for fee waivers** for the first 12-24 months on any account
  - **Ask about startup packages** not listed online ‚Äî branches sometimes have discretion
  - **Bundle personal + business** (especially at National Bank ‚Äî $1,313/year savings)
  - **Credit card annual fee waiver** for year one
  - **Overdraft protection** setup without fees initially

### 3. The Real Play
- **Open a $0 BMO eBusiness account** for day-to-day operations (no negotiation needed ‚Äî it's free)
- **Also open a National Bank Hybrid account** for the personal banking bundle savings
- **Later, when revenue comes:** Move to Float or Venn for interest earnings on idle cash
- **This costs $8/month total** and gives you two banking relationships

### 4. What Banks Actually Care About
- **Deposits** ‚Äî They want your money sitting there
- **Credit products** ‚Äî They want to lend to you eventually
- **Payroll** ‚Äî If you hire, running payroll through them is sticky
- **Merchant services** ‚Äî If you process payments, they want that revenue
- **Promise future business** ‚Äî "We're a tech startup expecting to scale. We want a banking partner who'll grow with us." This works especially well at National Bank's tech team.

### 5. Red Flags to Avoid
- Don't sign up for unnecessary products (overdraft you don't need, business credit line at high rates)
- Avoid long-term GIC commitments just to waive fees
- Don't let them upsell insurance products during account opening
- **Read the fee schedule** ‚Äî ask for a printed copy. Banks bury fees for things like returned items, stop payments, and wire transfers.

### 6. Branch vs. Online
- For a corporation, most banks still require at least one in-person visit for identity verification
- **BMO and TD** have the most advanced online onboarding for corporations
- **National Bank** requires branch visit for debit card but starts process online
- Bring everything on the first visit ‚Äî second trips waste time

### 7. The Documents Power Move
- Walk in with **everything organized in a folder**: incorporation docs, by-laws, resolution (use the bank's template from their website), two IDs, CRA BN, proof of address
- Having your act together signals you're serious and saves the advisor 30 minutes of fumbling
- If Alex doesn't have a CRA BN yet, **get it before going to the bank**
