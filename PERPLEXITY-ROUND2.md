## Prompt 2: IRS Impersonation Scams

### Key Statistics
- **FTC 2024:** $9.14M in IRS-specific losses (median loss $319), within $789M total government imposter losses
- **FBI IC3 2023:** Government impersonation falls within the $1.3B+ tech/support fraud category
- **Robocalls:** 33% of Americans report IRS as top robocall type; 56 billion total robocalls annually

### Common Scam Methods
- **Gift card payments:** iTunes, Google Play, Green Dot MoneyPaks
- **SSN suspension scam:** Full step-by-step social engineering attack
  1. Robocall claiming SSN compromised
  2. Transfer to "agent" who confirms personal details
  3. Demand immediate payment to "clear" the issue
  4. Instruct victim to buy gift cards and read numbers
- **AI variations:** Voice clones of IRS agents, deepfake official letters, surge during 2025 tax season

### How Real IRS Communicates
- Mailed CP notices (paper correspondence)
- CP5071C letter for identity verification
- **Never** calls demanding immediate payment
- **Never** threatens arrest over the phone
- **Never** requests gift cards or wire transfers

### Demographics Most Targeted
- Seniors (65+)
- Immigrants and non-citizens
- Non-English speakers

### Victim Stories
- Atlanta man lost $280,000 to IRS impersonation scheme
- Consumer lost $20,000 in gift cards in single scam session

### Recovery Steps
1. Report to FTC at reportfraud.ftc.gov
2. File IRS Form 14039 (Identity Theft Affidavit)
3. Place credit freeze with all three bureaus
4. Notify state tax agency

## Prompt 3: Zelle/Venmo Fraud

### Zelle Statistics
- **Volume:** $806 billion processed in 2023
- **Fraud losses:** ~$725 million (0.09% fraud rate)
- **CFPB finding:** $870M in losses at Bank of America, JPMorgan Chase, and Wells Fargo (2017-2024)

### Legal Actions
- **CFPB vs Zelle/EWS:** Sued December 2024, dismissed March 2025
- **NY Attorney General:** Ongoing since August 2025, alleging $1B+ in losses
  - Defendant: Early Warning Services (EWS), owned by JPMorgan Chase, Bank of America, Wells Fargo, Capital One, PNC, Truist, US Bank

### Common Scam Types
- **Accidental transfer scam:** Full step-by-step
  1. Scammer sends money (from stolen account)
  2. Contacts victim claiming "accidental" transfer
  3. Asks victim to "return" the money
  4. Original transfer reversed; victim loses their own funds
- **Bank impersonation / "me-to-me" scam:**
  - Scammer poses as bank fraud department
  - Instructs victim to send money to "themselves" to "secure" account
  - Example: $5,500 loss documented

### Reimbursement Landscape
- **Reg E coverage:** Unauthorized transactions only
- **Imposter/authorized scams:** Voluntary reimbursement by banks
  - Bank of America: 24% reimbursement rate
  - Chase: 2% reimbursement rate
  - Wells Fargo: 4% reimbursement rate

### Venmo-Specific Fraud
- Fake payment screenshots (edited images)
- Pending payments shown as "completed"

### Cash App
- **CFPB penalty:** $175 million ($120M consumer redress + $55M civil penalty)

### Canada Comparison
- Interac e-Transfer zero-liability policy is stronger than US Regulation E
- Canadian consumers have better fraud protection for peer-to-peer payments

## Prompt 4: AI De-Anonymization & Voice Cloning

### Attack Pipeline
1. AI agents scrape social media profiles
2. LLM synthesis aggregates data points
3. Persona crafting builds psychological profile
4. Sentiment scoring identifies vulnerabilities
5. Targeted delivery of personalized scam

### Key Statistics
- **456% rise** in AI-enabled fraud from 2024 to 2025
- **Voice cloning:** Requires only 3-30 seconds of audio (from TikTok, YouTube, voicemail)
- **70% of people** cannot distinguish cloned voices from real ones
- **Deloitte projection:** $40 billion in US fraud losses by 2027

### Victim Stories
- Houston father lost $15,000 after scammers cloned his son's voice in a fake kidnapping call

### Mitigation Strategies
- **Style obfuscation:** LLM rewriting of social media posts drops de-anonymization attacks to chance level
- **Platform mitigations:**
  - Rate limiting on profile access
  - Scraping detection systems
  - Content nudges (privacy warnings)
  - Post-processing noise injection on audio/video

## Prompt 5: Canadian Immigration Scams

### Study Permit Scams
- Always verify institution on DLI (Designated Learning Institutions) list
- Check Letter of Acceptance (LOA) format against known templates
- Legitimate institutions never require upfront fees to "agents"

### LMIA Scams
- Employers are **never** permitted to charge workers for LMIA processing
- Scammers demand $1,000 to $45,000 for fake or fraudulent LMIAs
- Workers should verify LMIA through ESDC portal

### Ghost Consultants
- Charge $10,000 to $50,000 for fabricated documents
- Forge LMIAs and Letters of Acceptance
- Operate without CICC (College of Immigration and Citizenship Consultants) registration

### Notable Enforcement Actions
- **Ontario — Singh:** $66,000 fine (largest individual penalty)
- **Amirahmadi:** License revoked + $32,000 fine + $50,000 costs
- **CICC 2025 crackdowns:** 18 penalized in Ontario alone, approximately $500,000 in total fines

### Verification Resources
- **CICC Registry:** Verify consultant registration status
- **ESDC Portal:** Verify LMIA authenticity
- **1-800-O-Canada:** Government information line

### Reporting
- **CAFC:** 1-888-495-8501
